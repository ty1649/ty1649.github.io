3-Wheeled Electric Go-Kart

Skills Gained

CAD Laser Cutting Plasma Cutting FDM 3d Printing Machine Shop Tools Cost Optimization Metal Fabrication

Description

My group and I decided to make a 3 wheeled electric go kart as a part of an engineering class. We were offered wheels, the makerspace, 80/20 extrusions, round stock, an electric motor, and a tiny amount of money to make it work. We also decided to make our go kart pirate ship themed -- which meant it had to be pointy at the front, hence the 3 wheels!

Summary

We started off with dividing the work between us for different subassemblies, which included the

  • Chassis
  • Steering
  • Drivetrain
  • Cockpit (Gas/Brake Pedals)
  • Integration

With this division of tasks, we all collaborated on a top assembly and finished our CAD (with over 130 components in the total assembly).

Since we couldn't afford a U-Joint, we used a bevel-gear based system for single wheel steering in the front, fastened to the chassis with 3d printed parts and fasteners, and a custom plasma cut gusset for concentric alignment. Our chassis featured 80/20 gussets that were FEA analyzed, and secured together with more plasma cut gussets and fasteners. Our battery powered 2 BLDC motors, one per rear wheel, which were connected with belts and a determined ratio via the FDM 3D printed drivetrain components to the wheels. Our brakes engaged with a bike brake that pressed against the wheels when activated. We made our chassis as skinny/short as possible, based off the measurement of our shortest friend, to ensure weight savings.

Finally, to stay in theme, we had our pedals have a pirate symbol be imprinted on them, our steering wheel be a pirate ship wheel that we CAD-ded, and our drivetrain components to 'look' like beefy tridents (while passing the testing under max torque).

We had to take special note to make sure that the components we were designing were able to be manufactured/made, or bought cheaply, and when assembling was possible to be able to be put on. This was a big issue in some chassis components, which we had to re-design -- but in the end, we got everything on the kart and it worked!
