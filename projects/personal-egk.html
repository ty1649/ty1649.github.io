3-Wheeled Electric Go-Kart

Skills Gained

CAD Laser Cutting Plasma Cutting FDM 3d Printing Machine Shop Tools Cost Optimization Metal Fabrication

Description

Situation: I, along with my group, started a project to make a 3-wheeled electric go-kart as part of an engineering class. We were provided with wheels, access to a makerspace, 80/20 extrusions, round stock, an electric motor, and a limited budget. Our theme was a pirate ship, which inspired the pointy front design and the three-wheel configuration.

Task: My role involved dividing the work among team members for different subassemblies, including the chassis, steering, drivetrain, cockpit (gas/brake pedals), and integration. I collaborated on the top assembly and contributed to the CAD design, which comprised over 130 components.

Summary

Action: I took the initiative to design a bevel-gear-based system for single-wheel steering in the front, using 3D printed parts and fasteners, along with a custom plasma-cut gusset for alignment (we couldn't afford a U-Joint). I also worked on the chassis, which featured FEA-analyzed 80/20 gussets, secured with plasma-cut gussets and fasteners. I designed the drivetrain components, which included FDM 3D printed parts, to connect the battery-powered BLDC motors to the rear wheels. The brakes were designed to engage with a bike brake system. I ensured the chassis was as compact as possible for weight savings, based on the measurements of our shortest team member.

Result: The project was a success, with the go-kart fully assembled and operational. The pirate theme was maintained with custom-designed pedals, a pirate ship wheel for steering, and trident-like drivetrain components. I learned the importance of designing components that are manufacturable, affordable, and easy to assemble. Despite some redesigns, particularly in the chassis, the final product met all our objectives and functioned as intended.
