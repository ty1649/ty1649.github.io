Xenopus Embryo Sorter Design

Skills Gained

Mechatronics Microfluidics SLA Printing FDM Printing PDMS Mold Design Hardware Integration

Description

Xenopus eggs often come in batches of hundred/thousands, and each egg needs to be checked and filtered out if they're useless. This was previously done manually, with hours of tedious labor. As the sole mechanical engineer, I designed the hardware components for this mechatronics project, which automates this sorting process.

Summary

The eggs that are being sorted are these ~1mm wide eggs, that will flow through a chip, and through a combination of sensors and pressure regulators, will be stopped, photographed on both sides, sorted with a machine learning model, and then when they're flowing out, sensors will keep track of which egg goes where, and they'll be seperated from each other with a quake valve sorter mechanism (which was selected from iterating through other sorting mechanisms).

There will be a transparent chip made of PDMS, which is casted from an SLA printed mold, and a housing that positions this chip in betweeen two mirrors, so when a camera is pointed it can take a picture of both the top and the bottom of the chip for the machine learning model to categorize the eggs.
