Fingerprint Door Unlocker

Skills Gained

Arduino 3D Printing DFM/DFA CAD Mechatronics

Description

Situation: I kept losing my key card, and since each was $20, I wanted to find a different, better solution to unlock my dorm door.

Task: I needed to design a mechanism that could move my dorm handle 40 degrees, or a linear distance of around 4 inches, with about 1.5 foot-pounds of force.

Summary

Action: I started by measuring and planning the specifications for my project. I decided to use an old DC motor found in the junk, which spins at 550 RPM. To increase the torque, I implemented a lead screw linear actuation system. I designed and 3D printed components, fastened with m5/m3 screws, and used a bearing and an L298N motor driver to power the motor. The assembly was coded on Arduino and mounted onto the door with command strips. To account for the circular motion of the door handle, I created an end effector with a claw and a print-in-place hinge.

Result: The device successfully opened the door in less than 3 seconds. However, I couldn't find many pictures of the project working before having to move out.
