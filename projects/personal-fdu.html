Fingerprint Door Unlocker

Skills Gained

Arduino 3D Printing DFM/DFA CAD Mechatronics

Description

I kept losing my key card, and since each were $20, I wanted to find a different, better solution to unlock my dorm door.

Summary

I started by getting the specs for my project -- after some measurement, I had to somehow mount a contraption that could move my dorm handle 40 degrees, or a linear distance of around 4 inches. Additionally, it has to turn the door handle with about 1.5 foot pounds of force (very inaccurate maybe?). After considering options, I settled on using a old DC motor I found in the junk, and since it supplied a questionable amount of torque, I would bump that up through a lead screw linear actuation system. The DC motor spins at 550 RPM, which I calculatd would take < 3 seconds to open the door.

With these initial specifications planned out, I began designing -- I just 3d printed some components, fastened together with m5/m3 screws. I dug up a bearing, and also bought an L298N motor driver to power the DC motor. The whole assembly was coded on Arduino, and mounted onto the door with command strips to the necessary force rating.

Finally, since this device pushes linearly, but the door handle turns in a circle, I created a end effector that features a claw with a print in place hinge, where even though the device pushes straight, the claw turns in a circle to follow the door handle and apply force without allowing slipping.
