MK10 Sprocket and Chain Assembly

Skills Gained

CAD Modeling FEA Analysis Topology Optimization DFM/DFA GD&T Manufacturing

Description

As the Sprocket Responsible Engineer for Bruin Formula Racing, I was tasked with designing the sprocket and chain assembly, which included the

  • Front/Rear Sprocket
  • Transmission Shaft
  • Chain

Summary

Slides for Preliminary Design Review (PDR), Critical Design Review (CDR), and Final Design Review (FDR), showing the evolution of the front sprocket, rear sprocket, and motor shaft over the course of the past couple of months:

Action:

  • I used SolidWorks to model the sprocket teeth geometry for a 59-tooth rear sprocket and a 12-tooth front sprocket, based on final drive ratio values simulated on OptimumLap.
  • The involute splines at the center of the sprockets and on the motor shaft were also modeled to ensure proper alignment and functionality.
  • To reduce weight, I ran a Topology Optimization on the rear sprocket using Altair Inspire and iterated over cutouts.
  • Static simulations on Ansys Mechanical were conducted to verify that both sprockets passed with a 2x factor of safety, based on chain tension calculations.
  • For the motor shaft, I selected a 17 teeth 17 DP 45-degree pressure angle spline and maximized the bore hole cutout, ensuring it didn't fail using hand calculations derived from maximum shear stress and normal stress theories.
  • A smaller chain, the 428VX, was selected over the previous year's 520 chain, allowing for closer sprockets and a larger FDR, which simulations indicated would maximize points.

Result: The project resulted in a well-optimized sprocket and chain assembly that met all design specifications and safety requirements. The new design contributed to improved vehicle performance and efficiency, demonstrating significant weight reduction and enhanced integration with other components.
