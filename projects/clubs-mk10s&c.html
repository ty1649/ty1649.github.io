MK10 Sprocket and Chain Assembly

Skills Gained

CAD Modeling FEA Analysis Topology Optimization DFM/DFA GD&T Manufacturing

Description

As the Sprocket Responsible Engineer for Bruin Formula Racing, I was tasked with designing the sprocket and chain assembly, which included the

  • Front/Rear Sprocket
  • Transmission Shaft
  • Chain

Summary

Slides for Preliminary Design Review (PDR), Critical Design Review (CDR), and Final Design Review (FDR), showing the evolution of the front sprocket, rear sprocket, and motor shaft over the course of the past couple of months:

I used SolidWorks to model the sprocket teeth geometry for a 59 tooth rear sprocket, and a 12 tooth front sprocket (based of final drive ratio values simulated on OptimumLap). I also modeled the involute splines that are at the center of the sprockets, and on the motor shaft. The sprockets were designed to sit parallel in the top assembly, which is optimal for chain tension.

I then used Altair Inspire to run a Topology Optimization on the rear sprocket, and iterated over cutouts on this piece to reduce weight. I verified that both the front and rear sprockets passed with a 2x factor of safety with static simulations on Ansys Mechanical, based off equations to calculate chain tension.

To optimize the motor shaft, I iterated over and selected a 17 teeth 17 DP 45 degree pressure angle spline, and maximized the bore hole cutout. I analyzed this part to make sure it didn't fail, mainly using hand calcs derived from maximum shear stress theory and maximum normal stress theory.

A smaller chain (after analysis was done) was also selected this year, the 428VX isntead of the 520 chain from last year, which allowed for closer sprockets and a larger FDR, which according to simulations should maximize our points.
