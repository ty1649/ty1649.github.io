Hydrogen Fuel Cell

Skills Gained

CAD Modeling CFD Analysis Thermal Analysis CAM Programming DFM/DFA Project Management Technical Documentation Team Leadership

Description

As the Hydrogen Lead for the UCLA Supermileage Vehicle team, I have to integrate a hydrogen fuel cell to work along with the rest of car and the systems and supply continuous power to the wheels. I'm also leading the initiative to design, analyze, and manufacture our own PEM open cathode fuel cell.

Summary

Since creating a Hydrogen Fuel Cell requires both chemistry/materials science and mechanical engineering knowledge, we've split up this subteam into two seperate teams: MEA (which handles the chemistry/matsci) and Hardware (which handles the Hardware components and analysis).

The MEA team is currently using blade coating techniques and reading literature to coat catalyst onto the membranes in conjuction with the Huang Lab, which we've been working with. Through trying various recipes out, we're trying to save cost and manufacture our own membranes with catalyst in house.

The Hardware team is designing and analyzing the various components in the fuel cell, with various factors to keep in mind, including uniform mass transfer throughout the flowfields, cooling and heat management, humidity, and clamping force within the stack.

As the lead of both these teams, I provide guidance on any troubles these teams are running into with analysis -- along with determining the overall trajectory of our subteam, and the general specs of the fuel cell we're trying to develop, including parameters like operating temperature, pressure, expected power, current, active area, etc. I also handle the communication and integration with sponsors and other teams.
