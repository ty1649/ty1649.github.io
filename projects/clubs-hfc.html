Hydrogen Fuel Cell System Design

Skills Gained

CFD Analysis Project Management CAM Programming Technical Documentation Team Leadership

Problem Statement

As the Hydrogen Lead for the UCLA Supermileage Vehicle team, I needed to design and implement a hydrogen fuel cell system that could meet our 1 kW power requirement for the competition vehicle. The system needed to be efficient, safe, and properly integrated with the rest of the vehicle while passing strict technical inspections.

My Work

Led multiple project teams through the complete development cycle:

  • Managed and coordinated 3 project teams consisting of over 30 members, strategically dividing work based on individual strengths
  • Conducted detailed CFD simulations using Ansys Fluent to optimize the flowfield design for effective water removal and mass transfer
  • Created CAM programs with HSMWorks for in-house manufacturing on our 3-axis CNC machine
  • Oversaw the successful integration of the Horizon Fuel Cell with the competition vehicle
  • Developed comprehensive documentation using Notion workspace and presentation slides for knowledge transfer

Results

The hydrogen fuel cell system achieved several key milestones:

  • Successfully met the 1 kW power requirement for the competition vehicle
  • Passed all hydrogen technical inspections for competition compliance
  • Reduced manufacturing costs by over $200 through in-house CNC production
  • Optimized flowfield design for efficient water removal and mass transfer
  • Established robust documentation system for future team reference

Lessons Learned

This project provided valuable insights into:

  • Managing large teams and coordinating multiple concurrent projects
  • Importance of simulation-driven design in fluid systems
  • Balance between cost reduction and system performance
  • Value of comprehensive documentation for knowledge transfer
  • Technical considerations for hydrogen system safety and compliance
